Well, when I first started university, I was so unsure of myself and my abilities. For instance, in first year, I could barely wrap my head about git commands, but now I actually prefer using git on the terminal vs. a user interface! Essentially, this section of my website serves as a reminder to myself and everyone that failures are normal.

I also read others' failure resumes and it helps me alot, so I hope my failure resume can help you too.

2021

Did not get nominated for the Palantir Women in Technology Scholarship :(

Rejected as a speaker/workshop host for Superposition V.

I took that criticism pretty badly, and for the rest of the meeting I was unable to say anything else because I could not stop thinking about it. After reflecting, I realized this criticism wasn't targeted at me, but rather the project I was working on. One thing to always remember: You Are Not The Code You Write.

I really hated the course content, and on the last day of the drop deadline, there was one seat left in STAT 251 (that I should of taken), but I stayed enrolled in Environmental Microbiology. If you don't enjoy a course, don't feel bad for dropping it. Your time and money is valuable.

After rediscovering my love of chemistry, I was SO excited to find a club like UBC iGEM. However, when I opened up the application, I realized that I had put in the deadline date wrong; it was 2 days ago.

This one hits hard because I was planning to major in Chemistry, and it was my favorite course in high school. Since I took Chemistry HL as an IB student, I was able to skip first year chemistry, which meant I was quite rusty when I took CHEM 233, more than a year after my IB exams. For the future, I'll make to revisit my foundations!

2020

This worked out pretty well because I rediscovered my love for chemistry! After taking some biology and chemistry courses during the summer I decided to minor in biochemistry.
